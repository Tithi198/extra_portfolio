Evolution of Aircraft Flight Data Recorder Methods and Technology (Black Box)

Institution: Associated with University of South Wales

Date: Nov 2024 - Apr 2025

The development of Flight Data Recorder (FDR) technology has played a key role in improving aviation safety, accident investigations, and operational efficiency. This study examines the evolution of FDRsâ€”from basic mechanical devices in the 1950s to modern digital systems with greater storage and durability. Despite improvements, current FDRs face limitations in data retrieval during crashes in remote or underwater locations, as seen in cases like Air France 447 and MH370.

Using a quantitative approach, surveys from 112 aviation professionals revealed moderate awareness of current FDR systems and concern over issues such as data access and safety. Respondents showed strong support for emerging solutions like real-time transmission, cloud storage, and AI integration, though concerns about regulations, cybersecurity, and costs remain.

The research concludes that while FDR technology has advanced, further improvements are needed. Key recommendations include boosting awareness, aligning international regulations, enhancing cybersecurity, and investing in real-time data solutions.

RC Aircraft

Institution: Associated with United College of aviation science & management

Date: Aug 2023 - Feb 2024

The aim is to teach beginners the basics of flight, such as lift, thrust, and drag, using easy-to-fly RC training aircraft. It also seeks to introduce learners to different types of aircraft and engine systems through hands-on flying experience. Additionally, the goal is to provide an enjoyable and challenging hobby that helps build skill and confidence in controlling small aircraft.

The outcome analysis shows that the RC trainer aircraft demonstrates strong aerodynamic stability, allowing it to fly effectively even in tough conditions. It is lightweight, as expected, making it well-suited for training purposes. The aircraft can fly for up to 30 minutes, though the average flight time is around 20 minutes. Its maximum control range is 1 km, which is slightly below expectations, but this may vary depending on the environment (as tested in Bangladesh). Additionally, the RC aircraft can achieve a moderate airspeed, although the specific speed in mph was not provided.

Quadcopter Drone

Institution: Associated with United College of aviation science & management

Date: Nov 2022 - Mar 2023

The objective of a quadcopter project is to design and build a remotely controlled flying vehicle. A successful outcome results in a fully functional quadcopter capable of performing a range of tasks, including surveillance and monitoring, aerial photography and videography, payload transport and delivery, autonomous flight missions, and emergency or fire response operations.

The specific outcomes of the quadcopter project include the ability to remain airborne for up to 30 minutes and carry a payload exceeding 1 kilogram. It is capable of autonomous flight without human control and may be equipped to extinguish fires and help prevent their spread. Additionally, the quadcopter can be utilized for parcel delivery, enabling faster and more efficient transportation of items. While the specific objectives and outcomes may vary depending on the focus and requirements of the project team, all efforts share the common goal of creating a reliable, remotely controlled flying system.

The aim of the project is to explore and develop drone technology for a wide range of practical applications. This includes conducting in-depth research into drones and their uses, as well as planning and designing the technical aspects required to construct a fully functional quadcopter. A key goal is to create a drone that can be operated remotely through wireless communication. The project also aims to enable autonomous drone delivery services for items such as food, packages, and medical supplies. In addition, drones are intended to be used for emergency rescue operations in disaster zones where human access is difficult or dangerous. Agricultural applications are another major focus, including field surveys, seeding and spraying, livestock monitoring, and crop yield estimation. The project also supports wildlife conservation efforts by providing a cost-effective and safe method for tracking and monitoring animal populations. Lastly, the drone will be used to support both professional and recreational aerial photography through high-quality imaging.
